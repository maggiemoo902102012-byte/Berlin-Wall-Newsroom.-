Berlin Wall Falls: East and West Germany Reunite

Thousands celebrate as the symbol of Cold War division is opened after 28 years.

By Historical Affairs Correspondent | November 10, 1989
Crowds gather around the Berlin Wall as border restrictions are lifted.

Berlin witnessed extraordinary scenes last night as East German authorities announced that citizens would be allowed to cross into West Berlin. Within hours, thousands gathered at checkpoints and along the Berlin Wall, celebrating what many believe marks the beginning of a new era in Europe.

Since its construction in 1961, the Berlin Wall had stood as one of the most powerful symbols of the Cold War, physically and politically dividing East and West Germany. Families, friends, and communities were separated for nearly three decades.

Following months of political unrest and growing calls for reform across Eastern Europe, pressure mounted on East German officials. Yesterday's announcement led to emotional reunions as people crossed the border freely for the first time in years.

Political leaders across the world welcomed the development. Analysts suggest the event could accelerate democratic reforms throughout Eastern Europe and reshape the balance of power on the continent.
